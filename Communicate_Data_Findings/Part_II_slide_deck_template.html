Part II - Reporting Carrier On-Time Performance¶

by Abdullah S. Othman¶

Investigation Overview¶

The goal of this presentation is to explore the key factors influencing flight delays and identify actionable insights to improve airline punctuality. The analysis highlights critical relationships between delays, carrier performance, and operational factors.

Executive Summary¶

  1. Departure Delays Drive Arrival Delays: A strong positive correlation shows that addressing departure delays can significantly reduce arrival delays.
  2. Carrier Performance Varies: Some carriers consistently manage delays better, while others exhibit more variability and outliers.
  3. Flight Distance Has Minimal Impact: Delays are influenced more by operational and environmental factors than by flight distance.

Dataset Overview and Executive Summary¶

Dataset Overview¶

  • Source: Airline performance data with information on delays, carriers, and flight operations.
  • Key Features:
    • Arrival Delay: Time difference between scheduled and actual arrival.
    • Departure Delay: Time difference between scheduled and actual departure.
    • Carrier: Airline operating the flight.
    • Flight Distance: Distance covered by the flight.

Summary of Findings¶

  1. Departure delays are the main driver of arrival delays.
  2. Some carriers manage delays more effectively than others.
  3. Flight distance does not significantly impact delays.

This summary provides the foundation for the key insights and visualizations presented.

Departure Delay vs. Arrival Delay

  • There is a strong positive correlation between departure delays and arrival delays.
  • Addressing departure delays is critical for minimizing arrival delays.
  • Most data points cluster near zero, indicating that the majority of flights experience minimal delays.

plt.figure(figsize=(10, 6))

    data=Flights_df, 
    x="DepDelay", 
    y="ArrDelay", 
    alpha=0.3
)  # Plot with transparency for better readability
plt.title("Relationship Between Departure and Arrival Delays")  # Add title

plt.show()
